I've been employed to code for {{fullYearsSince}} years 5. Of which, I've done ML engineering or research for {{dsYears}} years.

It is some career. So far I have worked on a precision methadone pump, a website generator for bed & breakfasts, secure off-site credit card storage, procurement streamlining for e.g. 'aerospace' companies, a metre-precise 3D model of Britain's rivers, a performance dashboard for use by secondary school teachers, medical risk scoring models, countermeasures against machine-learning model extraction...

I fell in love with the technical quite late. I was working for the stats department of the Scottish Government and watching the local longbeard run something simple like PCA on childhood poverty data. Here was something which didn't rely on the assumptions of the humans using it (or really not many anyway). Here was a way out of the endless woods of philosophy - or rather, a way to a superior philosophy, beyond merely verbal philosophy.

More superficially, I was blown away when I saw the gov's sysadmins slouching through the crowd of suits in their Behemoth and Orange Goblin t-shirts. There was power, enough to ignore irrelevances and puffery.

I now do "research", which means I rarely write anything more than a script or maybe a few gnarly stats functions. Besides this!
