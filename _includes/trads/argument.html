1. On the face of it, I should love the life of the liberal arts. Why don't I?
  2. Some psychological reasons of course - chiefly contrarianism and impatience.
  3. But some real reasons too:
    Differing trust in the Burkean selection of texts
    Differing trust in individual/current-group rationality
    Without rationalists, the traditionalists' canon would be much smaller
    The Kantians among them cannot will their behaviour as a universal maxim
    The time-inconsistency of their attitude to future Great Books bothers me
    At this point we have a 250 year tradition of tearing down fences and coming out well on net.
    It's wrong to withdraw from the world.
