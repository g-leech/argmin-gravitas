- Full sincerity: aligned, clear access, inform, mutual sincerity, all objects
- Lying: misaligned, clear access, deceive, asymmetric, propositional
- Self-deception: misaligned, no access, inform (thinks), mutual, propositional
- Aspiration: misaligned on current state, clear access, signal/perform, ambiguous, identity
- Kink: often misaligned, clear access, perform, mutual fiction, attitude/identity
- Bullshit (Frankfurt's technical sense): indifference to truth-value, rather than commitment to falsehood. The bullshitter doesn't care whether what they're saying is true.
- Irony/sarcasm: statement-belief mismatch, but with the expectation that the audience will recover the true meaning. Cooperative insincerity.
  
- Kayfabe: maintained fiction where all parties know it's fiction but treat it as real within the frame. Professional wrestling, some political theatre. Related to your kink example but without the erotic dimension.
  
- Performative politeness: "How are you?" / "Fine." Nobody thinks this is a health inquiry. The sincerity conditions are different from the surface grammar.
  
- Bad faith (Sartre): self-deception specifically about one's own freedom or facticity. The waiter who is "too much" a waiter.
- Confabulation: sincere-seeming justifications generated post-hoc for actions driven by other causes. You believe your explanation, but it's causally disconnected from the behaviour.
- Preference falsification (Kuran): publicly stating preferences you don't hold, knowing others are doing the same, with collective awareness that the stated preferences don't track private ones.
- Motivated reasoning: intermediate between sincerity and self-deception—you arrive at beliefs through biased processes, but the beliefs are genuinely held.
- 
- **1. Belief-statement alignment**
  
    - Does the speaker believe the proposition they're asserting?
        - Aligned → misaligned → indifferent (bullshit)
- **2. Metacognitive access**
  Does the speaker know their own actual belief/intention/motivation?
    - Clear access → partial → confabulating → self-deceived
- **3. Communicative intent**
    - What is the speaker trying to achieve in the audience?
        - Inform → deceive → perform → shibboleth / signal ethos → simple mimicry
- **4. Common ground**
    - Does the audience know the epistemic status of the utterance?
        - Mutual knowledge of sincerity → mutual knowledge of fiction (kayfabe) → asymmetric (one party deceived) → ambiguous
- **5. Object of (in)sincerity**
  What is being (mis)represented?
    - Propositional content → attitude/emotion → identity → intention
